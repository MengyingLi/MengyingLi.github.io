Projects

I use statistical tools to evaluate my research question and visualization techniques to deliver my answer. I love both static and interactive visualization. Most of my static plots are made in R (ggplot) and Python (seaborn) and my interactive visualizations are made with various JavaScript library, such as Leaflet and D3.

Interactive Visualization Projects

  • Below is a list of some recent interesting interactive visualization that I've worked on and all of my projects are accessible from Github.
  • Match Point: Left or Right? Mengying Li
    In this visualization, I used R to webscrape all the basic information, such as handedness and turned pro, from ATP Single Ranking . Then I ranked all the left-handed and right-handed players based on their frequency in the US Open and visualized the games played between them using D3.js
  • Chinese Restaurants in the City of New York Mengying Li
    In this work, I first used QGIS to geocode all the locations of the Chinese restaurants. I got the demographic information for each neighborhood of NYCfrom American Community Survey and visualized the distribution of both Chinese restaurants and the distribution of percentage Chinese in total population using Leaflet. We can see that the largest density of Chinese restaurants is in Chinatown, Flushing and Sunset Park West, which are also the home to a large portion of Chinese.
  • 2009 US Open Ring Network Mengying Li
    In this visualization, I presented the story of how Del Potro defeated all the more experienced opponents and became a black horse using D3plus.
