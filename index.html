Projects

I use statistical tools to approach my research questions and visualization techniques to present my answers. I love both static and interactive visualization. Most of my static statistical plots are made in R (ggplot2) and Python (seaborn) and static maps in ArcGIS. My interactive visualizations are made with various JavaScript libraries, such as Leaflet.js and D3.js.

Interactive Visualization Projects

  • Below is a list of some recent interesting interactive visualizations that I've worked on. All of my projects are accessible at Github.
  • Match Point: Left or Right? Mengying Li
    In this visualization, I used R to webscrape basic information about tennis players, such as handedness and when they turned pro, from ATP Single Ranking . Then I ranked all the left-handed and right-handed players based on their frequency of participation in the US Open and visualized the results of the games played between them using D3.js
  • Chinese Restaurants in the City of New York Mengying Li
    In this work, I first used QGIS to geocode all the locations of Chinese restaurants in NYC. I got the demographic information for each NYC neighborhood from American Community Survey and visualized the distribution of both Chinese restaurants and the percentage of Chinese people in the total population for each neighborhood using Leaflet.js. We found that the largest densities of Chinese restaurants are in Chinatown, Flushing and Sunset Park West, which are also home to the greatest concentrations of Chinese people.
  • 2009 US Open Ring Network Mengying Li
    In this visualization using D3plus, I presented the story of how Del Potro, a dark horse, defeated all the more experienced opponents.
  • McGraw-Hill Internship Final Presentation for Steven Laster Mengying Li
    This is the shiny app that I developed during my summer internship at McGraw-Hill Education, to show the results from classification models to predict whether a student would pass/fail using Engrade Data, when I was doing a summer internship at McGraw-Hill Education in 2014. I gave a presentation to our Chief Digital Officer, Steven Laster, using this visualization. Click on "Feature Selection" and "About" tabs for more background graphics.
